# Stakeholder Discovery Guide — D-NAMO Inventory Tracking

**Purpose:** Interview each role to understand current as-is workflow before designing the system.
**Note:** Team has not started using Procore yet. Focus entirely on current manual/Excel/WhatsApp process.
**Date:** 2026-03-05
**Session:** brainstorming-session-2026-03-05-1200.md

---

## Role 1: Logistics Officer (On-Site)
*Goal: Understand the current delivery receiving and logging process*

1. Before a delivery arrives, how do you know what's coming? (WhatsApp message? Email? Printed PO? You just wait and see?)
2. Who tells you a delivery is scheduled, and how far in advance do you usually know?
3. Walk me through exactly what you do from the moment a truck shows up until it drives away — step by step.
4. What documents do you collect from the GC or driver? (Delivery note, invoice, packing list, material certificate?)
5. What do you do with those documents? (Take a photo? Scan? Hand them to someone? File them somewhere?)
6. How do you record that a delivery happened — Excel, notebook, WhatsApp message, nothing?
7. Who do you notify after a delivery is logged, and how? (WhatsApp the PM? Email someone in HQ?)
8. What happens when a delivery is short, damaged, or wrong material? What do you do and who do you tell?
9. Have you ever received materials that weren't expected — no one told you they were coming?
10. How do you know if a delivery was already counted or if it's a duplicate? Has that ever caused a problem?
11. After receiving, do you track where the material goes on site, or just that it arrived?
12. What's the part of this process that creates the most problems down the line?

---

## Role 2: Storekeeper (On-Site Stock)
*Goal: Understand how on-site stock is managed day to day*

1. How do you know what materials are physically in the store right now? Notebook? Excel? You just know from memory?
2. When an engineer or worker needs materials, how do they request them from you? (Come in person? WhatsApp? Written form?)
3. How do you record what you've issued — to whom, how much, for what, on what date?
4. What happens when materials come back to the store? Is there a return process or does it just get put back?
5. Do you reconcile at the end of the day or week — check what's been issued vs. what's still physically there?
6. Do you report your stock levels to anyone? How often and in what format?
7. What are the most common problems you run into? (Things going missing, wrong quantities, no one telling you something was taken?)
8. If your notebook or Excel disappeared tomorrow, what information would be gone forever?
9. What's the most time-consuming thing you do every week that you wish you didn't have to?
10. If one thing could be automatically tracked for you, what would it be?

---

## Role 3: Engineer (Consumer/Requester)
*Goal: Understand how materials are requested and consumed from the engineer's perspective*

1. When you need materials for a task, how do you get them? Walk me through it.
2. Do you know what's available in the store before you ask, or do you have to go check?
3. When something you need isn't on site yet, how do you find out when it will arrive?
4. Have you ever had work stopped or delayed because materials weren't there when you needed them? How often does that happen?
5. Do you track what materials you've used for a specific activity or location? Or is that someone else's job?
6. Does anyone ever ask you to account for what materials you used? In what form?
7. What's the most frustrating materials-related problem you deal with?

---

## Role 4: Project Manager (Visibility & Control)
*Goal: Understand how the PM tracks materials and what visibility they currently have*

1. Right now, how do you track what's been ordered vs. what's arrived vs. what's been used on a project?
2. Where does that information live — one person's head, an Excel file, WhatsApp threads?
3. What's on your ideal weekly materials status report — what do you need to know?
4. When something goes wrong (delayed shipment, wrong material delivered, shortage on site), how do you find out? Who tells you and how?
5. Who approves material purchases or purchase orders on your projects?
6. Do you have visibility on materials sitting in the warehouse that haven't been sent to site yet? How?
7. What do you report to GDH management about materials, and how painful is it to compile?
8. What's the single biggest inventory or materials problem that has cost a project money or time?

---

## Role 5: In-Country Warehouse / Logistics Coordinator (if this role exists)
*Goal: Understand how goods are managed between arrival in-country and dispatch to site*

1. When goods clear customs and arrive at the warehouse, what happens? Walk me through your receiving process.
2. How do you track what's currently in the warehouse — quantities, condition, which project it belongs to?
3. When a site requests materials from the warehouse, what's the process to get them dispatched?
4. How do you confirm that what you sent actually arrived at the site?
5. Do you handle stock for multiple projects or sites from the same warehouse?
6. How long do materials typically sit in the warehouse before going to site? Is there ever confusion about what's ready to ship?
7. What paperwork or records are generated when goods leave the warehouse?
8. What falls through the cracks most often in your current process?

---

## Role 6: Procurement / Supply Chain (whoever handles ordering)
*Goal: Understand the ordering and in-transit tracking side*

1. When you place an order with a supplier, how do you track its status until it arrives?
2. How do you know when something has shipped — does the supplier tell you, or do you have to chase?
3. Do you track expected delivery dates? Where? How often are they accurate?
4. When a shipment is delayed, who finds out first and how does the information flow to the site?
5. How do you match what was ordered against what actually arrived and was invoiced?
6. Do you ever have items partially delivered across multiple shipments? How do you track that?
7. What documents come with a shipment that you need to verify and file?
8. What's the information gap that causes the most problems between ordering and the site actually having the material?

---

## General (ask everyone)

- What tool do you use most right now to communicate about materials? (WhatsApp? Excel? Email? Paper?)
- What information about materials do you wish you had but don't?
- What's the data that gets lost most often in the current process?
- Have you ever had a dispute about whether something was delivered or not? How was it resolved?
- If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about how materials are tracked, what would it be?
